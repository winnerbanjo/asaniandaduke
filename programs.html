Our Programs & Impact

Providing structured pathways for skills acquisition, cultural enrichment, business development, and healthcare support.

Empowerment Paths

Programs, Services, & Training

We structure our interventions into core areas designed to achieve long-term growth and sustain cultural heritage.

Àdìrẹ Value-Addition

Hands-on textile production training in traditional resist-dye methods (Àdìrẹ Eléko, Àdìrẹ Alábẹ́rẹ́, Àdìrẹ Oníko) and commercial value-addition strategies.

Business Workshops

Workshops helping artisans grow from local micro-operators into viable exporters, run in strategic partnership with ITF, SMEDAN, NEPC, and TAFTA.

Youth Empowerment

Capacity-building bootcamps helping youths tap into the creative industry, providing technology, startup kits, and enterprise education.

Women Livelihood Programs

Tailored vocational training designed for women and widows, supporting them with micro-loans, marketing access, and self-reliance skills.

Vocational Skills Acquisition

Broad vocational learning catalogs including fashion design, tailoring, crafts production, and packaging to boost general employable skills.

Cultural Heritage Advocacy

Educating students, public bodies, and heritage enthusiasts on traditional Yoruba dyeing history, design motifs, and African craft preservation.

Mentorship & Coaching

Connecting our training graduates with seasoned entrepreneurs and creative designers to guide them through market scaling and business growth.

Community Health & Inclusion

Organizing medical check-ups, health education workshops, and economic inclusion initiatives for underserved communities where our artisans reside.

Creative Industry Support

Nurturing creative hubs, promoting local fashion innovation, and helping indigenous artisans access modern e-commerce channels.

Exhibitions & Festivals

Hosting public awareness events, craft festivals, and local/international exhibitions. Highlights include the annual International Àdìrẹ and Cultural Festival.

Demographics

Who We Support

Our foundation primarily focuses on enabling individuals and communities who can benefit from structured creative enterprise development.

01

Youths & Aspiring Entrepreneurs

Young individuals seeking practical vocational skills to construct self-reliant careers and start creative enterprises.

02

Women Seeking Self-Reliance

Women interested in financial independence, managing business entities, and establishing home-based craft lines.

03

Students & Schools

Educational institutions seeking cultural curricula and hands-on workshops in indigenous textile preservation.

04

Artisans & Creatives

Existing micro-crafters looking for modern styling, branding, and export-readiness mentorship to enter global markets.

05

Underserved Communities

Impoverished neighborhoods receiving vocational outreach programs and direct health checkups.

06

Cultural Practitioners & Enthusiasts

Heritage advocates and practitioners seeking collaborative platforms, exhibitions, and advocacy circles to celebrate traditional crafts.
