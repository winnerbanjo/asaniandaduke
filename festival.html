International Àdìrẹ & Cultural Festival

Amplifying Africa's rich textile heritage, supporting local artisan communities, and promoting sustainable craft entrepreneurship on the global stage.

A Celebration of Craft

Sustaining the Threads of Yoruba Heritage

The annual International Àdìrẹ and Cultural Festival is the signature event of the Àṣàní and Àdùkẹ́ Foundation. Hosted in Abeokuta, Ogun State—the historic heartland of Àdìrẹ textile production—this festival serves as a vital platform to showcase the incredible talent of local artisans, women dyers, and young fashion entrepreneurs.

By integrating traditional Yoruba dye methods (like indigo resist-dyeing) with contemporary fashion aesthetics, the festival bridges the gap between historical heritage and modern global markets. It attracts fashion designers, cultural researchers, international delegates, and art enthusiasts from across the globe.

What to Expect

Festival Highlights & Interventions

Experience a vibrant blend of historical preservation, creative innovation, and direct community empowerment.

Art & Craft Exhibitions

Explore an immersive exhibition of heritage textiles, featuring unique designs from veteran artisans. Discover the history and symbolism woven into traditional Yoruba resist patterns, from classical motifs to contemporary art translations.

Àdìrẹ Runway & Fashion Show

Watch international and local designers display their latest Àdìrẹ collections. The runway highlights the versatility of indigenous textiles, demonstrating how they can be adapted into modern, global high-fashion apparel.

Live Dyeing Masterclasses

Participate in hands-on workshops led by master artisans. Learn the delicate processes of pattern creation (Àdìrẹ Eléko with cassava paste resist, and Àdìrẹ Oníko with tie-dye methods) and mix your own natural indigo dyes.

Cultural Panels & Business Talks

Engage with policymakers, fashion industry experts, and export facilitators (like SMEDAN and NEPC) discussing creative entrepreneurship, securing funding, and expanding African crafts to global audiences.
